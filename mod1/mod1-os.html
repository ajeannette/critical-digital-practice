We interact with computers today via 3 main operating systems: Windows (Microsoft), macOS (Apple), and Linux (open source).

The Operating System (OS) is a platform on which your computer's hardware and the applications running on it can exchange information. Some people like to imagine the OS as your computer's government. As analogized by John Kubiatowicz in a lecture on OSs, one can consider their OS the "coordinator and traffic cop" that "manages all resources... settles conflicting requests for [those] resources" and "prevents error and inappropriate use of the computer" (2010). Furthermore, the OS facilitates "what everyone needs" by supplying "standard libraries" and "windowing systems." Without an OS, a typical computer has a truly unmanageable amount of resources, so having an OS in place is essential to keep everything under control and actually useable.
